Master's Thesis: Di-Jet production at Hadron Collider

Under the guidance of Dr. Meduri C. Kumar

Collider experiments have made remarkable contributions to particle physics, including precise measurements like the coupling constant of Quantum Electrodynamics (QED). They have also led to key discoveries such as quarks and gluons, with the most notable being the Higgs Boson found at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC), enhancing our understanding of particle mass.
Collider experiments act as a backbone for testing the Standard Model and for the search for "New Physics". This report provides an analysis of a particular collider experiment. Its primary objective is to examine the production of di-jets at hadron colliders, quantify the scattering cross-section associated with this process, and identify practical applications for further investigations into various phenomena in the field of physics. In this presentation, I will introduce the theoretical framework for calculating cross-sections in particle physics. We will discuss local gauge symmetry and examine scattering experiments that reveal the structure of protons.
I will then explain asymptotic freedom and confinement, essential concepts for understanding quark and gluon behavior. Finally, we will explore jets and their leading-order calculations, connecting theory with experimental observations to illustrate key insights into the subatomic realm. We study numerical methods for calculating the jets’ cross-section and develop a computational program for this process. Finally, I discuss possible applications of the Collider Experiment.

Bachelor's Project: Solution to Laplace and Poisson Equations

Under the guidance of Dr. Syed Rashid Ahmed

Developed numerical solutions for Laplace’s and Poisson’s equations using finite difference (jacobi) and successive relaxation methods. Implemented the algorithm in fortran, optimizing iterative convergence for 2d potential fields. Computed electric potential and electric field distributions in cartesian coordinates for given boundary conditions. Analyzed convergence behavior and the impact of charge density on potential variation across grids. Documented the methodology and results in a detailed project report as part of the computational physics course.
